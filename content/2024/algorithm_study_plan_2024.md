## Study Strategy

1. **Pattern-first, not problem-first** — Group by technique (sliding window, two pointers, DFS/BFS, DP). Recognizing the pattern is 80% of the solution.
2. **Frequency-weighted priority** — Master the high-frequency problems first; they cover the most transferable patterns seen across companies.
3. **Spaced repetition** — Re-solve each problem after 3 days, 7 days, and 30 days. Write the solution from scratch each time — no copy-paste.
4. **Explain aloud** — Verbalize the approach and complexity analysis as if in an interview. If you can't explain it clearly, you don't understand it.
5. **Complexity triage** — For every solution, write down time and space complexity before coding. This locks in Big-O intuition.
6. **Edge case enumeration** — Before submitting, list 3–5 edge cases: empty input, single element, maximum bounds, negative values, duplicates.
