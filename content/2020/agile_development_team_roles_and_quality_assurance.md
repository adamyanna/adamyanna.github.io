# Agile Development: Team Roles & Quality Assurance

> 2020-09-22

Roles in an agile team and how QA extends beyond testing to connect every phase of the delivery lifecycle.

## Team Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Business Owner** | Industry customer; defines requirements |
| **Developer** | Builds and delivers the product |
| **End User** | The person the product serves |
| **BA** (Business Analyst) | Understands and extracts customer needs; translates them into Acceptance Criteria (AC); bridge between Business Owner and Developer — maximizes business knowledge transfer |
| **QA** (Quality Analyst / Quality Assurance) | Ensures system robustness across functional, security, and performance dimensions per AC |

## QA Is More Than Testing

QA's responsibility is not to passively receive AC and test after development is done. To solve the **requirement attenuation** problem caused by insufficient engagement during development:

- **Engage early** — When BA analyzes user stories and breaks down tasks, QA should already prepare environments, test strategies, and test data
- **Pair with developers** — Co-author automated test cases during development, not after
- **Verify locally** — When development is complete, QA can quickly validate all acceptance conditions in the developer's local environment
- **UAT support** — Help Business Owner sign off during User Acceptance Testing

QA acts as a **connector** linking every participant across the product lifecycle, ensuring quality from inception to delivery — a far broader scope than "tester."
